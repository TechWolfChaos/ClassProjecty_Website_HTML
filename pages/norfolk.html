Facts about Norfolk


  1. The Great Fire

    2. On 1776 Lord Dunmore shelled the city of Norfolk for more than eight hours with his fleet of three ships. The combination of gunfire and fires started burned the city down with only the walls of Saint Paul's Episcopal Church surviving.


  2. Naval Base

    3. Home of the worlds largest naval base, Naval Station Norfolk


  3. Military

    4. Long history as a strategic Military and transportation point, where many railroads started.


  4. Yellow Fever

    5. On June 7th, 1855 the ship Benjamin Franklin brought yellow fever to Norfolk totaling of about 3,200 deaths.
